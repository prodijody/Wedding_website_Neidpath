"Latha na bainnse" is a beautiful Gaelic phrase that translates to "wedding day" or "the big day" in English. In Scottish tradition, this phrase carries the weight of centuries of celebration and commitment.

As the old saying goes: "Tha latha na bainnse a' tighinn a-mach!" — "The wedding day is coming!" These words have been whispered by excited couples across the Scottish Highlands for generations, carrying with them the anticipation, joy, and promise of a lifetime of love about to begin.

Venue

Visit Venue Website

Perched proudly beside the River Tweed in the Scottish Borders, Neidpath Castle has stood since the 14th century—its thick walls echoing with centuries of regal visits, romantic tales, and a ghost or two! Once a key stronghold of the powerful Hay family, it welcomed Mary, Queen of Scots, endured Cromwell's cannons, and inspired writers like Sir Walter Scott.

Legend whispers of the tragic "Maid of Neidpath," whose spirit roams its winding halls to this day. With its soaring tower, ivy-clad walls, and timeless views of rolling hills, Neidpath offers a uniquely enchanting setting—perfect for a wedding steeped in Scottish history and filled with stories your guests will never forget.

Guest Information

Directions

Neidpath Castle is located just outside Peebles in the Scottish Borders, approximately 23 miles south of Edinburgh.

Address: Neidpath Castle, Peebles EH45 8NW

The castle is easily accessible by car. From Edinburgh, take the A703 south to Peebles. When you reach Peebles, follow signs for Neidpath Castle.

Parking

There is ample parking available at the castle grounds for all guests.

Dress Code

The dress code for our wedding is formal attire. Ladies, please be mindful that parts of the ceremony and reception will take place on grass, so consider your footwear accordingly.

Things to Do

If you're planning to stay in the area for a few days, here are some local attractions you might enjoy:

  • Explore the charming town of Peebles with its local shops and cafes
  • Visit Traquair House, Scotland's oldest inhabited house
  • Enjoy outdoor activities in the Glentress Forest, including mountain biking and hiking
  • Tour the Scottish Borders and visit historic abbeys in Melrose, Jedburgh, and Kelso

Weather

Scottish weather can be unpredictable, even in July. We recommend bringing layers and potentially a light jacket or wrap for the evening, as well as an umbrella just in case.
